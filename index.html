Speaking of, when making a character in dnd, you should follow this order:

  1. First you should decide on your class. This is the most important as it effectively determines how you will play in combat and also outside of it
  2. Secondly is choosing your race/ species. Each race is unique with different things it can effect. while some races tend to be known for being certain classes, do not let that stop you from making the choice you want
  3. Next is then choosing your ability scores for your character. These can be done using the standard array, which are a set collection of numbers, or you can roll your stats which can add for more variation. Classes always have one or two stats they would prefer to have the highest number, so bear that in mind
  4. Additionally, decide on a background for your character, though this can be done after creating a backstory, or done before to give you something to help with making your backstory. Each background can help give certain proficiences
  5. Also, decide on how your character looks! Make them as unique as you want (While of course sticking to the appearance of the race you have chosen)
  6. And lastly, make a backstory! I find having one for your character makes you rather invested in their story. Make sure that it does fit in with the class you have chosen, but other than that, feel free to include details that you like, make them unique!

That is what you need to know in more simple terms, but if you're starting off then I am sure that the dungeon master you'll have will assist with all of those, except for the backstory at least, though they are obliged to tell you about the world in which your backstory will take place

Now regarding campaigns themselves, you can do an official module, published by those who do the other dnd books. These include:

  • Phandelver and below: The shattered obelisk
  • Baldur's gate: Descent into Avernus
  • Dungeon of the mad mage
  • And many others!

However you can also do your own or someone else's campaign that they have made themselves. These are generally called homebrew as they are not official and are made at home and can be a mix of official and made up parts

As of right now, i am a player in an offical campaign, but also a player in a homebrew campaign, both of which are very fun despite their differences, and also allow me to play as different types of characters, be it a different race, class, personality and backstory! in my opinion, it will be hard to get tired of playing
